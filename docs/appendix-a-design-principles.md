# Appendix A — Design Principles Summary

Ten principles that govern every decision across the Delivery Dashboard.

---

## 01. Objects Before Pages

Every important concept is an object. Pages simply display them.

Projects exist. Activities exist. Budgets exist. Pages are temporary. Objects are permanent.

---

## 02. Collaboration Before Isolation

Delivery is never performed by one individual. Every feature should support multiple contributors.

Ownership is individual. Progress is collaborative.

---

## 03. Typography Before Decoration

Typography is the primary visual language. Not colour. Not illustrations. Not gradients.

Good typography creates hierarchy. Hierarchy creates understanding. Understanding creates confidence.

---

## 04. Data Before Dashboards

The product exists to help users make decisions. Data is the hero.

Charts support decisions. Tables support decisions. Statuses support decisions. Decoration never takes priority over information.

---

## 05. Context Before Navigation

Users should rarely leave their current workflow.

Details open in drawers. Editing happens inline. Comments remain contextual.

Navigation should feel shallow rather than deep.

---

## 06. Reuse Before Duplication

Every component should justify its existence. If a pattern already exists, use it.

Build `ObjectCard`, not `ProjectCard`, `ActivityCard`, and `BudgetCard`.

---

## 07. Outcomes Before Activity

The platform does not exist to measure work. It exists to measure impact.

Completing work is more valuable than recording work. Milestones represent outcomes, not effort.

---

## 08. Performance Is A Feature

The fastest interface wins.

Every unnecessary click should be questioned. Every repeated workflow should be simplified. Every delay should be justified.

---

## 09. Accessibility Is Mandatory

Every screen, every component, every interaction must support:

- Keyboard navigation
- Screen readers
- High contrast
- Reduced motion
- Focus management

Accessibility is not a "nice to have." It is a requirement.

---

## 10. Consistency Builds Trust

Every table behaves the same. Every drawer behaves the same. Every badge behaves the same.

Predictability builds trust. Trust is the platform's most valuable feature.
