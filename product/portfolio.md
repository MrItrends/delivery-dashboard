# Portfolio

## Purpose

The Portfolio is the **strategic layer** of the Delivery Dashboard. It bridges strategy and execution.

Portfolio answers: *"How are our strategic priorities performing?"*

---

## Business Goal

Enable leaders to monitor, compare and govern multiple programmes from a single view while preserving the ability to drill into execution when necessary.

---

## Relationship

```
Workspace → Portfolio → Priority Areas → Projects → Interventions → Activities
```

Portfolio is never the destination. It is the **bridge between strategy and execution**.

---

## Portfolio Overview

Communicates: Total Priority Areas, Active Projects, Programme Health, Budget Utilization, Funding Gap, KPIs On Track, Milestones Completed, Cross-Programme Risks.

---

## Portfolio Health

Calculated — never manually assigned:
- Project Health, Milestone Completion, Budget Performance
- Target Progress, Delivery Velocity, Open Risks, Overdue Activities

---

---

## Portfolio Views

| View | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Overview | High-level strategic summary |
| Table | Dense comparison of Priority Areas |
| Timeline | Programme roadmap across time |
| Analytics | Performance trends and executive metrics |

Changing the view never changes the data.

---

## Cross-Project Dependencies

Users see: which projects depend on others, where delivery bottlenecks exist, which delays will cascade across programmes. Dependencies are **visualized**, not buried in reports.

---

## Executive Mode

Simplified interface for leadership: Summary-first, read-only by default, large comparative visuals, quick drill-down.

---

## Claude Implementation Notes

The Portfolio should feel like a **strategic command center** — not a spreadsheet.

Leaders should answer *"Where should we focus attention next?"* within seconds of opening the Portfolio.
